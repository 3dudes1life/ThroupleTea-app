Meet the Throuple Behind A Little Throuple Tea

We’re William, Caleb, and Daniel — three men in love, one real-life gay throuple, and the hosts of A Little Throuple Tea. Our podcast is where LGBTQ relationship stories, throuple life, chaos, humor, honesty, and chosen family all spill into one very caffeinated cup.

Who are the hosts of A Little Throuple Tea?

A Little Throuple Tea is hosted by William, Caleb, and Daniel of 3Dudes1Life. We are a real-life gay throuple sharing our relationship, our home, our creative projects, our pop culture meltdowns, our family dynamics, our business chaos, and the honest reality of what it looks like to build a life as three men in love.

This is not a podcast about pretending to have the perfect relationship. It is a podcast about being honest. We talk about communication, money, travel, jealousy, queer life, chosen family, haunted houses, reality TV, throuple arguments, and the funny little moments that make a relationship feel real.

We are not trying to be the official rulebook for every throuple. We are here to be honest about ours.

William

Host Chaos Manager Big Feelings

William brings the stories, the strategy, the emotional processing, and the “wait, we should turn this into a whole project” energy. On the podcast, he often steers the conversation while also somehow being the one who derails it.

As one-third of this real-life gay throuple, William talks openly about love, growth, leaving old chapters behind, building new dreams, and what it means to keep choosing a nontraditional relationship out loud.

Caleb

What makes our throuple different?

Every throuple relationship is different. Some are open. Some are closed. Some live together. Some do not. Some are private. Some are public. Our relationship is ours: three men building a home, a creative life, and a future together while learning how to communicate through everything real life throws at us.

We are a real-life gay throuple

This is not just a brand or a podcast concept. Our relationship exists off-camera, at home, in our routines, in our plans, and in the everyday details that never make it to social media.

We are three men in love

Our relationship is not about one couple plus a third person. It is a three-person partnership where all three of us matter, all three of us have a voice, and all three of us are part of the story.

We talk about the messy parts

Throuple life includes love and laughter, but it also includes conflict, jealousy, scheduling, money, family questions, and moments where communication has to get very real.

We built a podcast around honesty

A Little Throuple Tea exists because people are curious about LGBTQ relationships, nontraditional love, and what it actually looks like to share a life as three partners.

Why we started A Little Throuple Tea

We started A Little Throuple Tea because there are not many places where people can hear from a real gay throuple in a way that feels honest, funny, messy, and human. So much of what people know about throuples comes from assumptions, jokes, or oversexualized ideas. We wanted to show the actual relationship part.

The podcast gives us space to talk about what we are learning as partners, how our dynamic works, where we still struggle, what makes us laugh, and how being a throuple affects everything from travel to family to money to pop culture arguments on the couch.

It is part relationship podcast, part LGBTQ storytelling, part chosen family diary, part reality TV group chat, and part three men trying to explain their life before one of them makes it weird.

What we talk about on the podcast

  • Throuple relationship dynamics — communication, jealousy, boundaries, and conflict
  • LGBTQ relationships — queer love, identity, family, and community
  • Real-life chaos — travel stories, house stories, business stories, and everyday mess
  • Pop culture and reality TV — because the tea does not stop with us
  • Chosen family — the people, pets, memories, and inside jokes that shape our life
  • Honest conversations — the good, the awkward, the funny, and the parts people usually skip
